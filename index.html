Applied Causal SHAP · intervention-oriented explainability

Mediators steal the valor. We want to give it back upstream.

Traditional SHAP explains what a fitted model used. Our poster asks a different question: when credit pools on mediators and downstream markers, can causal structure recover the influential—and ultimately actionable—nodes that came before them?

The poster in one applied example

Prediction gives the medal to the last runner. Intervention needs the whole relay.

In the stylized poster DAG, Steroid affects pressure-injury risk largely through Inflammation and Oxygenation. Standard SHAP can place those mediators above the upstream intervention. We call that mediator credit transfer “valor stealing.”

Applied ICU translation

The same blind spot can reward a treatment marker.

In the poster’s MIMIC pressure-injury example, vasopressor use marks severe hemodynamic instability. A high SHAP value can therefore tempt the reader to treat the downstream clinical response as the causal driver. The intervention-oriented question is what upstream severity process drove both treatment and risk—and which part of that process can be changed.

Illustrative, not a new empirical result.

These values reproduce the stylized ranking used in the ACIC teaching experience. The renal benchmark below is the checked-in quantitative test. “Valor stealing” is a communication metaphor for mediator credit transfer, not a new estimand.

Influence, depth, recourse, and cost are different questions.

The proposed stack preserves those distinctions instead of asking one score to do four jobs.

Current quantitative test

The renal benchmark tells us which version of “causal” matters.

DAG ordering by itself does not reliably outperform a matched ordinary estimator. Structural intervention propagation does much better against frozen total-effect truth—provisionally. That is the bridge from the poster metaphor to the larger method stack.

Interpretation boundary

All current benchmark data are synthetic. LumaWarp, DiCE, and cost-sensitive DiCE are future layers in this repository. The page states the working thesis; it does not present the completed stack as validated.

The endpoint

Not a better explanation. A better first move.

Return credit upstream. Look deeper than the obvious mediator. Generate more than one feasible alternative. Then pay attention to what each alternative costs.
